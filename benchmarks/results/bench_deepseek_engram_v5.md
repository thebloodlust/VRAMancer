## Honest scope

- The lending buffer reservation and the P2P data plane between the 3090 and
  the 5070 Ti are infrastructure-level features. Once the buffer is allocated
  on the 3090 and the lease is ACTIVE, a `cudaMemcpyPeerAsync` round-trip
  measures real bandwidth — that's what the rerun will produce.
- The buffer is **not yet wired into vLLM's weight prefetch path**. vLLM owns
  its CUDA allocator in a spawned subprocess, and `cpu_offload_gb` keeps
  weights in pinned DRAM accessed via UVA. Routing weight prefetch through
  the 3090 buffer requires hooks vLLM does not currently expose — tracked as
  V6 work.
- For the data plane that **is** wired into VRAMancer's HF backend (multi-GPU
  models without TP=1), see `bench_lending_hetero_real.py` (Phase 2) — that
  benchmark exercises lending+ReBAR+P2P end-to-end during inference.

## Key insight

- Without VRAMancer: 5070 Ti OOMs on a 159 GB model; 3090 cannot run MXFP8
  (SM 8.6 < 10.0).
- With vLLM cpu_offload: the 5070 Ti handles all MXFP8 kernels, the 158B
  model runs (~0.5 tok/s, DRAM-bound by PCIe 4.0 reads).
- The lending pool will reserve 12 GB on the 3090 and validate a P2P data
  plane to the 5070 Ti once the bench is rerun with the Phase 1 fix.
  Real prefetch integration into vLLM is V6.
