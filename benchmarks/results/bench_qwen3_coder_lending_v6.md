## Honest scope

- The lending buffer is **reserved and P2P-validated**, but **not yet wired into vLLM's weight prefetch path**: vLLM owns its CUDA allocator in a spawned subprocess, and `cpu_offload_gb` keeps weights in pinned DRAM accessed via UVA. Routing weight prefetch through the 3090 buffer requires hooks vLLM does not currently expose — tracked as V6.E (`TURBO_KV_HMM_OFFLOAD` + lending integration for vLLM workers).
- For the data plane that **is** wired (VRAMancer HF backend, multi-GPU models without TP=1), see `bench_lending_hetero_real.py` — that benchmark exercises lending+ReBAR+P2P end-to-end during inference.

## Key insight

- Without VRAMancer: 5070 Ti (16 GB) cannot host the 30 GB FP8 model without spilling weights to DRAM, paying full PCIe latency on every expert miss; the 3090's 24 GB sit idle.
- With VRAMancer + vLLM cpu_offload: the 5070 Ti handles all FP8 compute on Blackwell, cold weights are UVA-offloaded to DRAM, and the 3090 reserves a P2P-validated staging buffer for V6.E weight prefetch.
- The lending pool reserves 13 GB on the 3090 and validates a 12 GB/s P2P data plane to the 5070 Ti — real prefetch integration is V6.E.
